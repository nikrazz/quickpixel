Leveraging Your Website: Insights and Tips for Small Businesses

Why Static Websites Are Faster Than Dynamic Websites?

  • 14 September 2023
  • Nikolai
  • Website Performance

If you’re a small business owner who’s considering a website, you’ve probably heard terms like “static” and “dynamic” websites thrown around.

But what do these terms mean and why does it matter to you?

In essence, the difference comes down to speed and simplicity.

  • Static websites are straightforward. Think of them as fixed displays in a shop window. They don’t change unless someone physically alters the display. They’re coded from the ground up, tailored to your needs, and they don’t rely on complex databases or functionalities. The result? Lightning-fast load times.

  • Dynamic websites, like those made on WordPress or Wix, are more like interactive displays. They change in real-time based on different parameters and user interactions. That might sound cool, but it also means they have a lot more going on behind the scenes which can slow things down.

Key takeaway: Static websites coded from scratch can load up to 10 times faster than those made with page builders like WordPress. Considering that most visitors will leave a site if it takes more than 3 seconds to load, speed matters. A lot.

Mobile-First Programming Boosts Speed

Here’s something you might not know: Google cares about how your website performs on mobile. And with good reason!

  • Most people today access websites from their mobile devices.

  • Mobile sites built with a page builder often aren’t optimised for speed.

  • They can be clunky, not entirely responsive, and can hinder your Google search rankings.

On the flip side:

  • Static sites are often coded with a “mobile-first” approach. This means they’re designed for mobile screens from the outset.

  • After ensuring optimal mobile performance, code for larger screens (like tablets and desktops) is added.

Key takeaway: A handmade static site ensures that your mobile site is responsive, optimised, and super fast. This can enhance your website’s ranking on Google and reduce the chance of visitors abandoning your site.

Static Sites Are More Secure Than Dynamic Sites

In the world of websites, simplicity often equals security.

  • WordPress, as powerful as it is, has its vulnerabilities.

  • Because it’s so popular, it’s a prime target for hackers. And its complex nature means there are more potential points of entry for malicious actors.

  • Static sites, on the other hand, are far simpler and don’t have databases that can be hacked or plugins that can be exploited.

Key takeaway: Handmade static sites are a fortress. Their simplicity and absence of common vulnerabilities make them a safer bet for your business.

Handmade Static Sites vs WordPress and Wix

Here’s a quick comparison:

  • Static Sites:

    • Tailor-made for your business

    • Faster load times

    • Mobile-first approach

    • Tight security

  • WordPress/Wix:

    • Templates might not capture your brand perfectly

    • Slower due to dynamic elements

    • Regular updates needed for security and functionalities

    • Known vulnerabilities

Key takeaway: While platforms like WordPress and Wix offer convenience, they might not provide the level of customization, speed, and security that a bespoke, hand-coded static website can offer. Making the right choice can significantly impact your business’s online performance and first impressions.

Paying a Professional Web Developer: Why?

  • Professional Expertise: Crafting a website is more than just choosing a template. It’s about optimizing images, ensuring fast load times, and ensuring a seamless user experience. Experts know the ins and outs of these processes.

  • Customization: While platforms like WordPress and Wix offer various themes, they might not perfectly capture your brand’s essence. A custom site ensures that every pixel aligns with your vision.

  • Time: As a business owner, your time is valuable. Instead of grappling with code or site builders, it’s often more efficient to have someone handle the technical side so you can focus on what you do best.

  • Long-Term Investment: Think of your website as a storefront. You want it to be welcoming, efficient, and secure. Investing in a professional website is investing in the long-term success of your business.

Key takeaway: By hiring a professional, you’re ensuring that your site is optimized, functional, and tailored to your brand’s unique needs.

Website As an Investment

Your website is often the first interaction potential customers have with your brand. It’s your online storefront, portfolio, and the face of your business. Here’s why investing in a quality website is paramount:

  • First Impressions Matter: Before they even read about your services, visitors will judge your business based on site design and speed.

  • User Experience: A fast, responsive site ensures a pleasant browsing experience, increasing the chances of conversions.

  • SEO Benefits: Search engines prioritize fast, optimized sites. A well-built static site can improve your rankings and visibility.

Key takeaway: Think of your website as an asset. Like any other investment, quality pays off in the long run.

Conclusion

In the digital age, your website acts as the digital storefront for your business. While dynamic platforms like WordPress and Wix have their merits, static, hand-coded websites often offer faster load times, heightened security, and a tailored user experience.

For small business owners looking to make a lasting online impression, investing in a professionally crafted static website might just be the edge you need.

Remember, in the vast world of the internet, every second and every pixel counts. Choose wisely!
